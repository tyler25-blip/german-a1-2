# Design Context

## Users

The primary audience is **judges evaluating an interactive music installation** — thoughtful, design-literate people who review many projects in one sitting. They skim first, read second. They are looking for: clarity of concept, originality of interaction model, and evidence of craft.

Context of use: desktop browser, during a judging session. Single-sitting, linear read-through. Not a returning audience — one chance to communicate.

**Job to be done:** In three minutes, understand exactly how this installation works, why the mechanic is interesting, and walk away with a clear memory of a single diagram or moment.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **contemplative, tactile, editorial.**

The piece should feel like a museum exhibit catalog — the kind with generous margins, a captioned illustration on every spread, and text you read slowly. It is NOT a startup pitch, SaaS landing page, or a product tour. No growth-hacked conversion funnels, no testimonials, no "features grid."

Emotional goals: quiet confidence, intellectual pleasure, the feeling of being trusted to understand.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** light, cool, near-white — not paper-toned, not warm. A tiny hint of blue in the neutral so the palette holds together with the blue atlas demos embedded inside. Clean but not clinical; never pure white.

**Visual principles:**
- **Visual-first, text-light.** Every concept earns a diagram. Body copy is short captions, not paragraphs. No long reading blocks.
- **Asymmetric editorial layouts.** Large negative space, offset columns, captions set to the side of illustrations like a museum booklet.
- **Tactile artifacts.** The Knob, Knob Wall, Table, Projection — each drawn as a palpable object in a specific scene, not iconographic abstractions. Illustrative quality, not flat UI icons.
- **Slow reveals.** Motion is restrained: gentle fades on scroll, never bounce or elastic easing, never more than one moving thing at a time.

**Color palette (OKLCH):**
- Background surface: `oklch(0.985 0.003 240)` — near-white with a faintest cool tint (not warm paper)
- Subtle surface / section fill: `oklch(0.96 0.005 240)`
- Primary ink: `oklch(0.20 0.018 260)` — deep, slightly-blue black
- Secondary ink: `oklch(0.48 0.012 255)`
- Rule / divider: `oklch(0.88 0.005 240)`
- Primary accent: editorial blue `oklch(0.48 0.18 258)` — confident, deep, never electric
- Secondary accent: softer mid-blue `oklch(0.62 0.12 240)` — for large shapes / shading
- **Gradients are allowed** — but only within the blue family (tonal blue-to-blue) or blue-to-neutral. Never blue-to-purple, never rainbow, never neon. Gradients should feel like ink dissolving in water, not synthwave.

**Typography:**
- **Bricolage Grotesque** (Google Fonts) used throughout, with variable optical sizing and weight axis.
  - Display / section headings: Bricolage Grotesque 600–700, optical size ~96
  - Body / captions: Bricolage Grotesque 400, optical size ~12–14
  - Small labels / metadata: Bricolage Grotesque 500 uppercase, tracked out, optical size small
- Bricolage was chosen because: it is a contemporary grotesque with genuine character (variable optical sizing, contextual narrowing), it is not in the skill's reflex-font ban list, and it carries editorial weight without being Inter/Plus Jakarta/Space Grotesk/Syne.
- **Absolutely avoid** any serif, any handwriting/display script, and the entire reflex ban list (Inter, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta, Outfit, Space Grotesk, Syne, Instrument Sans, IBM Plex, etc.).

**Anti-references (explicitly NOT this):**
- Not a startup landing page. No hero gradient, no three-column feature grid, no CTA buttons competing for attention.
- Not text-heavy. No paragraphs longer than ~40 words. Scannable by design.
- **Not blue/purple gradients. Not any gradient on text.** Blue is used only as solid fills or strokes. No purple anywhere in the palette.
- Not glassmorphism, not dark mode with glow, not rounded-rectangle cards with drop shadows, not neon.
- Not template-y. If a judge could mistake this for a Notion page, Figma site, or Framer template, it has failed.

## Design Principles

1. **The structure mirrors the mechanic.** The site is organized in two phases (Generate → Explore), exactly as the installation is experienced. Form enacts content.

2. **Diagrams carry meaning; text annotates them.** If a concept can be shown, it is shown first. Prose exists only to label or to give context that a picture cannot.

3. **One artifact per spread.** Each section of the page is anchored by a single, distinct visual — a knob in a hand, a table with three pucks, a projected map. No collages. No dense grids.

4. **Restraint over spectacle.** The piece earns attention through precision, not motion. One considered hover-state is worth ten animated gradients.

5. **Respect the judge's time.** Three-minute read, top to bottom. Every element must earn its scroll-distance or be cut.
